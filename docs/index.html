The claim under test

A seductive, expensive idea — checked where it could actually fail

Does a small loss of communication fidelity compound into a disproportionate, accelerating collapse (a squared penalty, E = U·D²), or does it cost only in proportion (E = U·D)?

If collapse truly spiraled, the rational response is to chase perfection at any cost. We tested the idea only where a valid, channel-intact two-hop measurement was possible — a yeast protein interactome and 992 pre-registered open-source repositories — with the falsification criterion fixed before the data were seen. In both, the squared term earns its keep in neither.

The evidence · two independent domains

A cell and a codebase, and they agree

Chosen because in both the outcome is measured, not assumed, and the two communication hops are genuinely independent (the channel-intact gate, VIF).

A dramatic earlier reading — that the quadratic was anti-predictive (AUC 0.41, below chance) — turned out to be a non-converged-fit artifact. Found, reproduced, and corrected: under any converging fit the quadratic simply doesn't help.

The warning light

One free number predicts collapse: τ v

Not how polished a system is — how fast it closes the risks it has already flagged for itself.

2.6×slower to clear its own backlog — the failing system's tell

Computable from records every organization already keeps: ticket queues, audit-finding logs, incident timestamps, bug trackers. The same construct re-instantiates for hospitals (root-cause-analysis closure), finance (audit remediation), and software (time-to-patch). A reference monitor ships in the repository.

Why it matters

Three durable gifts to technology & civilization

01

Stop fearing the cliff

Breakdowns cost in proportion, not catastrophically — so invest in many steady improvements, not ruinously expensive perfection. A wrong "everything spirals" prior quietly misdirects budgets in medicine, software, finance, and regulation.

02

Watch one free gauge

A rising backlog of unresolved, self-flagged risk is the earliest, cheapest, most universal sign of impending failure — in a hospital, a bank, a codebase, or a government.

03

Demand disprovable claims

The method's proudest credential is a pre-registered failure. It locked predictions before the data, retired its own false signals, and reported inconclusive as inconclusive — trustworthy precisely because it can be proven wrong.

Honest scope · part of the value

What LISM does not claim

  • The 50.6 / 19.8-day figures are specific to public repositories — not universal thresholds. Read the trajectory, not the number.
  • The relationship is correlational and probabilistic — a warning light, not an oracle. One input to human review, never an automated trigger.
  • Linear coupling is proven only in the two domains that admitted a valid test. Legislation and judicial coupling, run on live Congress.gov data, returned inconclusive — the required independent second hop isn't recorded anywhere reachable. Stated, not glossed.
