We propose the Telemetric Metric, d(i,j)^2 = kappa * tau_rt, in which proper distance is the round-trip information latency (commute time per unit coupling rate, equal to the effective resistance R_ij) between two regions of a correlation network, and the theory Latency-Metric Duality in which geometry is emergent bookkeeping of that latency rather than a fundamental container. We report a pre-registered Layer-1 numerical validation: the pre-registration spec (parameters, seeds, and pass thresholds) was locked under a canonical SHA-256 fingerprint before the run. Methodology: over 40 random correlation networks (N=6 nodes, kappa=1.0) we test three locked hypotheses. H1, the metric is genuine, yields 0 triangle-inequality violations out of 8640 checks. H2, the predicted scaling d ~ 1/sqrt(coupling), yields a log-log slope of -0.5000 with R^2 = 1.00000. H3, the primary discriminator, holds one pair of sites at fixed positions and sweeps only their information coupling: the emergent distance moves (range 0.94) while a fundamental-container null stays frozen (range 0). We report all endpoints against their pre-registered thresholds under a symmetric-null rule with no post-hoc adjustment. The physical experiment (a tunable-coupling qubit lattice or entangled optical-clock network) is proposed, not performed; the Layer-3 claim that physical spacetime is emergent is neither claimed nor proven. The measured sign and scaling of the coupling response is the falsifiable discriminator.
